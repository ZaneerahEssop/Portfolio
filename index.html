About Me

I didn’t always see myself in computer science. In fact, I started with little knowledge about programming or technology. What began as a curious choice quickly became a full degree—and now, in my final year, I couldn’t imagine taking any other path. My academic journey started with physics, which I loved but couldn’t fully commit to. I moved on to applied mathematics, and eventually pursued a double major in Computer Science. The transition from theoretical math to hands-on coding was challenging, but it taught me how to think logically, solve complex problems, and adapt quickly. Mathematics sharpened my analytical thinking, and that foundation continues to support my development as a programmer.What excites me most is how dynamic this field is—every week brings something new to learn. Lately, I’ve grown passionate about designing and building websites from the ground up, and I'm also exploring software development and data science. I plan to pursue an Honours degree to continue growing in these areas. From where I started to where I am now, it has been a long and demanding journey. But through perseverance, adaptability, and a strong work ethic, I’ve grown into someone who thrives in collaborative environments and embraces learning with enthusiasm. The challenges have been many—but so have the rewards.
